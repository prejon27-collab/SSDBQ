The White Man's Burden

Take up the White Man’s burden— Send forth the best ye breed— Go send your sons to exile To serve your captives' need...

To wait in heavy harness On fluttered folk and wild— Your new-caught, sullen peoples, Half devil and half child.

Take up the White Man's burden-- The savage wars of peace-- Fill full the mouth of Famine And bid the sickness cease;

‘The White Man’s Burden,’ is a lie they tell
While turning peaceful lands to hell
A man perched upon thrones of polished oak
dressed from head to toe in heavy silk;
To hide their immense greed beneath a righteous cloak
They claim their charity to be as pure as milk
The burden is a mask they wear
They called the darker nations ‘savage’ and blind
While leaving all ordinary human mercy behind
Condemning the savage through his lips stained with wine
Acting as vultures with a line and a chain
The savages are the ones consumed by greed—true swine who take more than they could ever need
Who ignore a fellow human’s desperate plea. The burden is a mask they wear. The savage is the one who sits in state and looks upon his brother with deep hate. The savage is not the one whom they have defined but instead the master with a cruel and narrow mind. The burden is a mask they wear. He counts dividends and carves the land, he boats of burdens -weight never truly felt- completely ignoring the pain his white hands dealt. He gifts a textbook to a mind they’ve already confined to heal the sickness only he had designed.

The African’s weary shoulder, bruised by the White Man’s lie.
