The White Man's Burden

Take up the White Man’s burden— Send forth the best ye breed— Go send your sons to exile To serve your captives' need...

Take up the White Man's burden-- The savage wars of peace-- Fill full the mouth of Famine And bid the sickness cease;

And when your goal is nearest The end for others sought, Watch sloth and heathen Folly Bring all your hopes to nought.

‘The White Man’s Burden,’ is a lie they tell while turning peaceful lands to hell. A man perched upon thrones of polished oak dressed from head to toe in heavy silk;
To hide their immense greed beneath a righteous cloak. They claim their charity to be as pure as milk. The burden is a mask they wear. They called the darker nations ‘savage’ and blind.
While leaving all ordinary human mercy behind. Condemning the savage through his lips stained with wine. Acting as vultures with a line and a chain. The savages are the ones consumed by greed—true swine.
The savage is not the one whom they have defined but instead the master with a cruel and narrow mind. The burden is a mask they wear.

He counts dividends and carves the land, ignoring the pain his white hands dealt. He gifts a textbook to a mind they’ve already confined to heal the sickness only he had designed.

The African’s weary shoulder, bruised by the White Man’s lie.
